Jainism

Jainism (/ˈdʒeɪnɪzəm/) is an ancient Indian religion that forecasts that its ontology, soteriology, and epistemology are true. The three main pillars of Jainism are ahiṃsā (non-violence), anekāntavāda (non-absolutism), and aparigraha (ascetism).
